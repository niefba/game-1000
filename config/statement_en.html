The Goal

Make more than 10,000 points.

  Rules

Players take turns.
The player receives 6 dices

If the dices are used to score points, you can either score the points or replay the dice.
To replay the dice, you must keep at least one of them.
If a dice roll fails to score points, all accumulated points are lost.

Each 1 is worth 100 points and each 5 is worth 50 points. A three of a kind is worth 100 times the value of the number on the dice, except in the case of a three of 1s, which is worth 1,000 points. Each additional number identical to the one making up a three of a kind doubles the value of the combination immediately below it.

  • A 5 is worth 50 points
  • A 1 is worth 100 points
  • Three 2s are worth 200 points
  • Three 3s are worth 300 points
  • Three 4s are worth 400 points
  • Three 5 are worth 500 points
  • Three 6 are worth 600 points
  • Three 1's worth 1,000 points

Four of a kind (four dice of the same value), five of a kind (five dice of the same value) and six of a kind (six dice of the same value) double the value of the combination just below them. For example, a three of 2 is worth 200 points, a four of 2 is worth 400, a straight of 2 is worth 800 and a six of 2 is worth 1,600. For a combination to count, it must be obtained in a single throw.

A sequence of 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 and 6 in a single throw is worth 1,500 points.

  Example

First roll of the dice : Player receives 6 dices with values 1 4 1 1 3 6
Player keeps all 1 value dice for a score of 1000 points providing to the standard output one line with 1 1 1

Second roll of the dice : Player receives 3 dices with values 2 5 6
Player passes for a score of 50 points and scores a total of 1000 + 50 = 1050 points providing to the standard output one line with pass

  Note

Warning: one turn allows the dice to be re-rolled a maximum of 2 times.

  Game Input

The program must first read the initialization data from standard input. Then, provide to the standard output one line with "pass" or a list of kept dices
Input

Line 1: an integer count, the number of rolled dices

Line 2: a string of integer values, the values of rolled dices

Output
A single line containing pass or a list of kept dices as a string of integer separated by spaces such as 1 1 5.
Constraints
The list of kept dices can't be empty.
